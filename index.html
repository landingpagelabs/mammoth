Our process

1.Discovery

It starts with a call with one of our founders, where we get to know your project, your space, and what matters most to you. From there, they walk your home with you in person, joined by one of our designers. Together, we put your budget behind what you'll value most, so the home is everything you want it to be, with nothing wasted. You'll come away with a clear proposal: the scope, the pricing and the timeline.

2. Design & Permitting

We start from your taste, how you live, and what you're drawn to. Then Maryana, our co-founder and design lead, works with our design team to bring your vision to life and take it further than you could alone, down to every detail: the layout, the finishes, the custom millwork. Once you've approved the design, we handle the co-op and condo boards, the alteration agreements, the DOB and Landmarks filings. We've worked with these boards for years and know precisely what each one requires, so we get them approved and keep it all off your plate.

3. Build & Move-In

Then Jessica, our co-founder and construction lead, runs the build with our own in-house crew and the specialty trades we've worked with for years. Someone from our team is on site full-time, making sure it's built exactly the way it was designed. You get frequent updates, and you walk the project with us at each key stage. At the end, our founders walk the whole home together to make sure every detail is right. Then it's cleaned and finished, and handed over ready for you to move into and enjoy for years to come.

Frequently asked questions

Do you handle the whole project, or do we hire a separate architect and contractor?

We do all of it, and the way we like to work is that one team owns the whole thing, the design, the permitting, and the build. You're not coordinating a separate architect, designer, and contractor yourself.

Our founders lead every project with a full team behind them, and we take real ownership of it with you, from the first walkthrough to the day you move back in. Because it's one accountable team from start to finish, what we design is what actually gets built.

Can you work within our budget?

We can, and we think our responsibility is to be honest with you about what your budget can really do, not to tell you what you want to hear. A lot of the early work is deciding what matters most to you and being realistic about the rest.

We price the real work by trade once we've seen the space, so what you're looking at is grounded, not a rough guess. Where something matters to you, we put the budget there, in finishes that will outlast all of us. Where it matters less, we'll say so, and set it aside so you can come back to it later without undoing anything.

Our minimum project size is $300,000. Given how hands-on we are on every home, and the design and craft we pour into it, that's the point where we can do the work justice and put your budget where it counts.

Do we have to deal with the DOB, permits, and our co-op or condo board?

No, that part is on us. We manage the filing and the expediting with the DOB, the Landmarks work when it comes up, and all the back-and-forth with your building. In practice that's mostly a conversation between our team and your building's architect or engineer, going back and forth until it's approved.

Your side of it is small, you sign your application on the DOB site and pay the city's fees, and we handle the rest. We only work in Manhattan and Brooklyn, and we've worked with these boards for years, so we know what each one wants to see. We'll keep you in the loop on where it stands, but getting it through is our job, not yours.

We've never renovated before and aren't sure what we want. Can you guide us?

Honestly, that's completely normal, and it's exactly what we're here for. You don't need to show up with the answer. Early on we'll do some exercises together to find the direction, we'll ask you for reference images and work from the ones you're drawn to, and we'll bring options to the table you might not have found on your own.

The way we think about it, our job is to take a rough idea and come up with a real strategy for the home you actually want, and take it further than you'd have gotten on your own. Then it stops being abstract. You'll see full drawings, renderings, and real material samples you can hold and compare before we build a thing.

Who actually does the work, your own crew or subcontractors?

Some of both, and we're happy to walk you through exactly who. The design is founder-led, with our in-house design team supporting, so a founder stays with your project the whole way.

The rough work, the carpentry and the general labor, is our own in-house crew, with someone from our team on site through the build. The licensed trades, the electricians, the plumbers, the HVAC, we sub out, but to people we've worked with for a decade. We're so careful about adding anyone new that it takes us forever, so the people on your job already know how we work.

How long does it take?

For a full gut, construction usually runs six to eight months, and we narrow that down once we understand exactly what we're doing. Design and permitting come before that.

Before we start building, we put together a detailed weekly schedule so you can plan your life around it. Once we're on site, you'll get a report from us every Friday, end of day, what happened that week, what's coming next, and a flag on anything that went sideways. We'll want you there in person for a few key moments, and at the end our founders walk the whole home together to make sure every last detail is right before we hand it over.

Do we have to move out during the renovation?

Yes, and we'd honestly recommend it. Trying to live through a full gut one room at a time means the whole job has to be staged around you, and people who go that way usually just prolong the disruption.

If you clear out, we can move through it in one stretch instead of working around you. We'll map the schedule in detail up front so you know exactly how long you'll be gone, and then you come back to a home that's finished and cleaned, ready to move into, not one you've been working around for months.
