Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Mammoth Projects (“Mammoth,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you visit our website or submit an inquiry, how we use it, and the choices you have.

Information We Collect

Information you give us. When you submit an inquiry, we collect what you choose to provide — your name, email address, the address of the property, and details about your project.

Information collected automatically. When you visit this site, we and the providers listed below automatically collect technical and usage information, including your IP address, browser and device type, approximate location, the pages you view, how you arrived here, and the advertisement or search you clicked. We also store advertising click identifiers (such as Google's gclid) in your browser so we can tell which advertising brought you here.

Session recording. We use Microsoft Clarity to record how visitors interact with this page. These recordings capture mouse movement, clicks, scrolling, and how you move through the inquiry form. They are used to understand where the page is confusing, and are not used to identify you personally.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above to:

  • respond to your inquiry and discuss your project;
  • prepare for and schedule consultations;
  • send you occasional news and updates, if you have opted in;
  • operate, maintain, and improve our website and services;
  • measure how our advertising performs and how visitors use this page;
  • show you Mammoth advertising on other websites and platforms after you have visited this one.

How We Share Your Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information for money. We do share it with the service providers and advertising platforms that help us run this site and our advertising:

  • Formspree — delivers your inquiry to us.
  • Google (Analytics, Ads and Tag Manager) — website analytics and measuring which advertising leads to inquiries.
  • Microsoft (Clarity) — session recording, as described above.
  • Meta (Facebook and Instagram), LinkedIn and Pinterest — advertising and re-targeting, which means these platforms may show you Mammoth advertising after you visit this page.
  • Ahrefs — website analytics.

We may also disclose information if we are required to do so by law.

Please note that under some privacy laws — including California's — using advertising and re-targeting cookies in this way is treated as “sharing” personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising, even though no money changes hands. See Your Choices & Rights below for how to opt out.

Data Retention

We keep your information only as long as necessary to respond to your inquiry and to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

This site uses cookies and similar technologies (including pixels, tags and browser storage). Some are essential to make the site work. Others are not, and we want to be plain about them:

  • Analytics cookies tell us how many people visit, which pages they read, and where they came from.
  • Advertising cookies are set by Google, Meta, LinkedIn and Pinterest. These do track your activity across other websites, and they allow those platforms to show you Mammoth advertising elsewhere on the internet.
  • Browser storage is used to remember the advertisement you clicked to get here, so we can tell which advertising works.

Your Choices & Rights

You may request to access, correct, or delete the personal information we hold about you by contacting us using the details below. You can also unsubscribe from our updates at any time using the link included in our emails.

To limit the tracking described above, you can:

  • block or delete cookies in your browser settings;
  • turn off personalised Google advertising at myadcenter.google.com, and opt out of Google Analytics with Google's browser add-on;
  • adjust ad settings directly with Meta, LinkedIn and Pinterest in your account preferences on those platforms;
  • opt out of interest-based advertising across many companies at once via the Digital Advertising Alliance;
  • contact us using the details below to ask us to stop sharing your information for advertising.
